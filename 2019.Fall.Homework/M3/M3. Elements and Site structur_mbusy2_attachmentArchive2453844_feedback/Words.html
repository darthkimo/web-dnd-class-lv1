Artistic philosophy

Art reveals itself as a means of reflecting and connecting, hence plays an extremely vital role in my life. It does not only serve as a canvas of expression, but also a mirror of retrospect. There is a healing nature in creating that I cannot find elsewhere; with every craft I've created, there is a certain honesty projected onto it. My artistic philosophy is simple; it is to continue intrepidly creating - there must not be a fear of producing "bad art," as each draft guides the artist closer to achieving the best possible result. My creative process in art differs to that in design. In the latter, which is also my field of study, I gather as much inspiration as I can. I analyze the subject of the design and study past works from notable artists and designers, assessing what suits my style and the notion I am trying to communicate. I believe that visual literacy makes a huge difference in creating meaningful pieces; it satisfies me to put in extra effort and thoughts into my designs and attaining a result with the quality I intended. My philosophy of creating without fear reflects on the way I approach my creative process; experiment and strive to grow from the first idea. Trusting the process enough to let go of ideals of what exactly the product must look like was previously a challenge for me. Nevertheless, it has also become one of the most imperative lessons in studying graphic design.
